A must-visit destination for tourists in the Philippines!

Are you undecided about where to go on your upcoming vacation? Don't worry; check these locations and choose what works best for you. From beaches, waterfalls, and architecture that have been conserved. Explore the world and discover inner bliss. To truly experience the Philippines, choose the ideal time to travel and the top destinations.


Luzon

Rizal Park

Rizal Park, also known as Luneta Park or simply Luneta, is a historical urban park located along Roxas Boulevard on Manila.

Intramuros

Intramuros, or the 'Walled City', is one of the oldest districts of Manila, built on the south bank of the Pasig River around 1571.

National Museum of Fine Arts

The National Museum of Fine Arts is home to 29 galleries and hallway exhibitions comprising of 19th century Filipino masters, National Artists, leading modern painters, sculptors, and printmakers.


Visayas

Tatlong Pulo Beach

Tatlong Pulo, or "Three Islands" in English, is a white-sand beach in Jordan, Guimaras. It gets its name from the three karst formations that face the beach.

Boracay

Boracay Island, one of the top beaches in the Philippines and one of the best Visayas tourist spots, is blessed with a long stretch of powdery white sand beach (called White Beach), crystal clear and azure waters, and a stunning sunset.

Chocolate Hills

The hills are located throughout the towns of Carmen, Batuan and Sagbayan and consist of about 1,776 mounds of the same general shape.

Mindanao

Aliwagwag Falls

This falls is known for its staircase-like boulders and is considered to be the highest waterfalls in the Philippines.

Maria Cristina Falls

The Maria Cristina Falls are often referred to as 'twin falls' as the flow of water is separated by a rock at the brink of the fall.

Mount Apo

Mount Apo, also known locally as Apo Sandawa, is a large solfataric, dormant stratovolcano on the island of Mindanao, Philippines.
