Oscar-Claude Monet


French painter Claude Monet (born November 14, 1840, Paris, France‚Äîdied December 5, 1926, Giverny) is recognized for his creation, leading, and wholeheartedly defending the Impressionist movement. In his mature works, Monet perfected his technique of creating series of repeated studies of the same topic, switching canvases according to the light or his evolving interests.



Faits sur Monet:

  • Monet is regarded as the "father of Impressionism" despite not being the sole artist in the movement because of his innovative work and constant dedication to the aesthetic.

  • Monet studied under various artists instead of going to a traditional art school. He first trained under a local artist before becoming friends with Eug√®ne Boudin, who introduced him to outdoor painting.

  • Color theory was a specialty of Monet's. From a distance, his quick, fractured brushstrokes of pure color would merge together to produce a vivid, lifelike scene.

  • Early in his career, Monet experienced financial difficulties despite his future fame. Painting sales were hampered by the art establishment's early rejection of Impressionist works.

  • Some people think Impressionism was affected by the development of photography. The Impressionists' emphasis on light and atmosphere was in line with photography's capacity to record brief moments.
